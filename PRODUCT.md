# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cinema Paradiso began as a tool for one power user managing a large local movie library, but it is now a public GitHub app for people with similar collections. The primary users are self-hosted Plex and local-media collectors who have hundreds or thousands of movie files, years of accumulated duplicates, unmatched folders, low-quality copies, and scattered discovery workflows.

They use the app on their own Windows machine, often while deciding what to clean, replace, discover, or watch next. They value privacy, speed, control, and a sense that their personal film archive is finally understandable.

## Product Purpose

Cinema Paradiso is a local-first movie library command center. It helps users clean duplicate files, inspect library quality, browse a large collection, cross-check Plex metadata, discover movies through TMDB and Prowlarr, and get local AI recommendations through Ollama.

Success means a user can trust the app to show the state of their library clearly, make cleanup decisions confidently, find something worth watching quickly, and stay in control of local files without cloud accounts, subscriptions, or opaque automation.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, powerful, sleek.

The interface should feel like a private film archive with real operational muscle: elegant enough to enjoy, dense enough to manage thousands of files, and calm enough that risky actions never feel chaotic. AI should feel like a tasteful curator inside the archive, not a chatbot gimmick.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, cheap Plex clones, purple AI-gradient apps, glassy or blurred crypto-style interfaces, beige premium templates, and cluttered torrent-indexer tables.

Avoid UI that looks generated by default: decorative gradient text, glassmorphism as a theme, identical icon-card grids everywhere, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section, oversized metric blocks, and visual effects that make library management harder to read.

## Design Principles

Make the user feel like they are commanding a private film archive.

Keep dense library workflows fast, scannable, and calm.

Use cinematic atmosphere without reducing clarity, contrast, or task speed.

Treat AI as a tasteful curator, not a chatbot gimmick.

Make risky actions like delete, rename, and cleanup feel controlled, explicit, and recoverable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast and interaction clarity. Body text, muted text, placeholders, badges, and controls must stay readable on dark cinematic surfaces.

The app should be keyboard-friendly for core workflows such as search, filtering, modal actions, cleanup review, and AI prompt submission. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. Color must not be the only way to communicate status; use labels, icons, text, or structure alongside color.
